The Problem

To get a quote, you overshare

01

Sellers broadcast confidential debtor files to every funder they ask.

02

Funders can see who else is bidding, and often the competing bids.

03

Platforms and brokers in the middle see everything, by default.

Receivables finance already prices on private, bilateral terms. Price competition and privacy pull against each other, and on a shared ledger, oversharing is the path of least resistance.
The Idea

A blind RFQ, scoped per party

SELLER

Publishes a Blind RFQ: attestations and economics, not raw debtor files.

FUNDERS

Submit Private Quotes only the seller can read, never each other, with funds committed behind every quote (a CIP-56 allocation): no bidding what you can't pay.

EVERYONE

Compliance, risk, coordinator and auditor each get their own scoped view.

Same RFQ, a different authorized view for every party, enforced on-ledger by Daml contract visibility, not filtered in a UI.

Party Interaction

How the RFQ moves between parties

01 · OPEN
Seller→ Funders ×3
  • RFQ Disclosure Package, attestations only, no raw files
  • Compliance + Risk attest eligibility on-ledger
›
02 · QUOTE
Funders→ Seller only
  • Private Quote with funds committed behind it (CIP-56)
  • Hidden from rival Funders & Coordinator
›
03 · SELECT
Seller
Seller Quote View
  • Picks the Best Compliant Quote
  • Funder identities & balances withheld
›
04 · SETTLE
Seller↔ Winning Funder
  • Payment and Receivable change hands atomically (CIP-56)
  • Winner identity revealed now
›
05 · AUDIT
→Auditor / Regulator
  • Scoped Compliance Receipt only
  • No full RFQ or Quote Book
Quotes are binding within their validity window; the fallback queue is seller-controlled, ordered by selection criteria, never auto-sorted by headline price.

Why Canton

No operator sees the whole book

  • A public ledger exposes every quote; a private marketplace means trusting an operator who sees everything.
  • Canton gives each party its own ledger view, so competitors and counterparties transact with no all-seeing middleman.
  • Disclosure is a ledger permission, so the compliance trail is real and selective, not reconstructed by a platform.
WHAT THIS DEMO IS, AND ISN'T

Settlement pays with a non-production Demo Settlement Asset through the CIP-56 token workflow.

Proof-of-Funds is a committed CIP-56 allocation: bid-eligibility evidence, not payment finality, a funds lock, or escrow.

No production custody, payments, or legal receivables assignment.
